Spain Closes Airspace Nationwide as Violent Riots Engulf Valencia

By Elena Torres, CNN 2 minute read | Updated 7:33 AM EDT, Thu June 19, 2025

MADRID (CNN) – In an unprecedented move, Spanish authorities have ordered the complete closure of the nation’s airspace. The decision comes in direct response to widespread, violent riots that have paralyzed the city of Valencia and overwhelmed local security forces.

The measure, which grounded all flights nationwide, was enacted to "prevent the escalation of the domestic crisis and maintain national security," according to an emergency alert from the Ministry of Transport. This drastic action effectively seals off Spain from international air travel.

The unrest in Valencia reportedly began as a peaceful protest against economic policies but escalated dramatically this afternoon. Eyewitnesses describe chaotic scenes as demonstrators clashed with police, setting fire to vehicles and vandalizing property in the city's historic center. Local authorities have requested national assistance.

The economic impact is expected to be substantial, with each hour of closure costing the aviation industry millions of euros. Eurocontrol, the European air traffic agency, is coordinating with Spanish authorities to manage the immense logistical challenge of the diverted flights.
